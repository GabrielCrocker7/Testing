To THE Madilyn Swavola

TRUE

What was that secret feeling none could know?
A fool I’d be, to claim that apple’s eye,
That myth, that art, a fable yet to show,
But cools my heart, each time I hear her cry.

To take for all there is, today is gold.
Yet flaw is virtue too, adored a while,
So fool I’ll be, to wrap her when she’s cold,
And stake in awe, its worth for just her smile.

To see her world, I’d sought, but lit a fire,
(Sick hearts like mine are seldom truly sane),
And watched, as she’d become my own entire.
For all she is, I pick to end this feign.

In all my thoughts of course, that her is you,
I know not what you’ll say, but know that this is true.
